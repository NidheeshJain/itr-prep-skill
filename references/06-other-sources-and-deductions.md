# Phase 4 — Other Sources, House Property, and Chapter VI-A by Regime

## Income from Other Sources (Schedule OS)

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Savings-bank interest | Taxable at slab. Sum per-account from AIS; cross-check bank certificates |
| FD/RD interest | Slab; taxed on accrual — AIS reports it yearly; TDS u/s 194A above threshold |
| **Interest on IT refund (244A)** | Taxable; appears in Form 26AS with the refund — the most-forgotten OS item; check whenever last year produced a refund |
| Dividend | Slab; report quarter-wise (ex-date determines quarter) for 234C relief |
| Family pension | Slab minus standard deduction for family pension (amount differs by regime — verify) |
| Gifts > ₹50k from non-relatives | Fully taxable u/s 56(2)(x); relatives/wedding/inheritance exempt |
| P2P/bond interest, EPF interest on excess contributions | Slab; check AIS |
| Lottery/online gaming | Flat 30% (115BB/115BBJ); no set-off, no deductions |

## House property (Schedule HP)

- Self-occupied (max 2): annual value nil; home-loan interest deduction (₹2L cap) **old regime only**.
- Let-out: rent − municipal taxes − 30% standard − full loan interest; but the resulting HP LOSS is
  capped at ₹2L against other heads (old regime) and **cannot be set off against other heads at all in
  the new regime** (carries forward within HP only).
- Co-ownership splits by share. Tenant TDS 194-IB appears in AIS/26AS — claim it.

## Chapter VI-A — regime availability table

| Deduction | Old | New |
|---|---|---|
| 80C (EPF/PPF/ELSS/LIC/tuition/principal, ₹1.5L) | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80CCD(1B) own NPS ₹50k | ✔ | ✘ |
| **80CCD(2) employer NPS** | ✔ | ✔ (the one big survivor — cap % of basic differs by regime, verify) |
| 80D health insurance | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80E education-loan interest (uncapped) | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80G donations | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80TTA savings interest ₹10k / 80TTB seniors ₹50k | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80U/80DD disability | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80EEB EV-loan interest (₹1.5L) | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80GG rent paid when no HRA received | ✔ | ✘ |
| 80GGC political donations | ✔ | ✘ | (non-cash only; heavily scrutinized — warn about bogus-donation notices) |
| 80CCH Agniveer | ✔ | ✔ |

80G fine print: cash donations capped (₹2,000); donee must have valid 80G registration + your donation
should appear in their filed Form 10BD (pre-fills into AIS) — claim only what matches.

When the user asks "where can I save tax" under the new regime, the honest answer is: almost nowhere
for the closed FY; the levers are prospective (employer NPS restructuring, salary structuring).
Don't invent savings. For old-regime filers, walk the table and ask for proofs.

## Clubbing and exempt income

- Minor child's income clubs with the higher-earning parent (₹1,500/child exemption).
- Income from assets gifted to spouse clubs back.
- Exempt income (PPF interest, agri ≤ threshold, LTCG within 112A exemption) still gets REPORTED in
  Schedule EI — exempt ≠ omitted.
- Agricultural income: exempt, but if > ₹5,000 AND total income exceeds the basic exemption, apply
  **partial integration** (agri income raises the slab rate on non-agri income; ITR-2/3 handles it via
  Schedule EI details) — and > ₹5,000 bars ITR-1/4.

## Schedule AL (assets & liabilities) — easy to forget

Mandatory in ITR-2/3 when total income exceeds the threshold (₹50L historically — verify current).
Report all assets at COST (land, buildings, vehicles, jewellery, financial assets, cash) and
corresponding liabilities as at 31 March. Users just under/over the threshold: check after the final
total-income figure lands, not at the start.
